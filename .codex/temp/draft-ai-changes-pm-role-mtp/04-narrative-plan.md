## Main conflict

Execution (docs, prototypes, “first draft code”) is getting cheap. That doesn’t remove PM work — it changes the bottleneck:
- from *producing artifacts* → to *choosing direction, designing constraints, and owning outcomes*.

## Expected behavior change (“what readers do next sprint”)

1. Shift from PRDs/hand-offs to a **hypothesis pipeline** with explicit kill criteria.
2. Start acting as a **full‑stack builder** for *prototypes and learning loops* (not “ship everything alone”).
3. Add an **AI quality loop** (evals + monitoring) as a product artifact, not an ML-only concern.
4. Treat **context** (data/permissions/freshness) as a product surface with ownership.
5. Make **security + cost** part of product definition-of-done and roadmap trade-offs.

## Hook variants (3)

### 1) Shock hook

Start with a concrete datapoint (YC W25 AI codebases) → reframe: “shipping is cheap now” → promise: “a next‑sprint playbook for PMs.”

### 2) Story hook

Open with a realistic PM moment: “I can vibe-code a prototype in an afternoon… but I don’t trust it.” → show the new job: “own the decision + constraints + learning loop.”

### 3) Tension hook

“Vibe coding makes it easier to ship. It also makes it easier to ship the wrong thing faster.” → set up the article as an operating system, not a trend piece.
