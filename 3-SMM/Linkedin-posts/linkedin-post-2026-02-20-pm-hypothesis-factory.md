# LinkedIn Post: PM as Hypothesis Factory Operator

12 months ago, a PM shipped features.
Today, a PM operates a hypothesis factory.

Here's the shift most product leaders are missing:

The old PM tested 2-3 hypotheses per sprint. Wrote specs. Ran standups. Waited for eng to build.
The new PM runs 30 experiments in the same window.

Not because they work harder. Because AI agents do the building, the prototyping, the data crunching.

The PM's job? Decide WHICH hypotheses to test. Then orchestrate a fleet of agents to test them — fast.

Think about it: 90% of features shipped at most companies are dead on arrival. Not because teams are bad. Because the testing loop is too slow.

When you can kill a bad idea in hours instead of weeks, everything changes. You stop debating opinions in meetings. You start running experiments before lunch.

The PM becomes an operator. Not a project manager. Not a feature factory worker. An operator of a hypothesis machine.

And the teams? They're shrinking. 3-4 people doing what 30-50 did two years ago.

This isn't a threat to your career. It's the biggest upgrade the PM role has ever seen.

The PMs who learn to orchestrate agents will be unstoppable. The ones who keep writing Jira tickets will wonder what happened.

Which side are you building for?
