# LinkedIn Post: CEOs Are Learning to Vibe Code

A CEO of a major insurance company just asked me to teach him vibe coding.
Not his team. Him personally.

Here's what I'm seeing right now: a real wave of AI adoption is hitting the executive level. Not "we should look into AI" memos. Actual CEOs, CPOs, and VPs sitting down with Claude Code and building things with their own hands.

Why now? Because something changed in November. Anthropic shipped Opus 4.5 inside Claude Code, and vibe coding went from "interesting experiment" to genuinely useful. AI agents actually work now. The output is real.

And executives are the ones feeling it most — because they've been stuck the longest.

Think about it. You're a leader with 10 ideas. Your dev team has capacity for 2 this quarter. The rest die in a backlog. Not because your team is bad — because development is inherently slow and expensive.

This isn't about bad IT departments. It's about the physics of software: everything takes longer than you think, costs more than you planned, and competes for limited engineering bandwidth.

Now imagine: you describe what you want in plain English. An AI agent asks clarifying questions. 30 minutes later — a working prototype. You show it to your team and say: "This is what I mean. Now let's build it properly."

The before/after is real:
→ Before: pitch idea → wait for estimate → fight for roadmap → 3 months → get something different
→ After: build a prototype before lunch → show engineering → iterate together

Leaders aren't just excited. They're relieved. The bottleneck they've lived with for years is finally breaking.

This isn't about replacing developers. It's about unblocking everyone else.

What's the one idea you've been waiting for dev capacity to test?
