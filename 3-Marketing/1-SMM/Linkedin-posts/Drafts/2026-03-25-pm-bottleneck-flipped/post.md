# PM Bottleneck Flipped

Being a PM in 2026 is a completely different job.
The hard part isn't shipping anymore — it's deciding.

The old model was simple: 1 PM manages 3-6 developers. Need more output? Hire more devs. The bottleneck was always people — their cost, their availability, their ramp-up time.

That bottleneck just flipped.

When you have 6 AI coding agents running in parallel in your terminal, the constraint isn't engineering capacity. It's your ability to figure out what to build next, think it through clearly, and own the consequences.

And you can't hire your way out of this one. Decision quality doesn't scale with headcount. You can't delegate business judgment to a new hire. The bottleneck is squarely on you.

Look at what's happening to team structures. The old setup — PM + designer + marketer + 4-5 developers — is compressing fast. Today, PM + half a designer + a quarter-time marketer + 1 developer ship MORE than that entire team used to.

Here's a real example. Ilya Izmailov, a full-stack builder on my team, rewrote in 1.5 months what previously took a team of 8 and cost over $100K. His setup: solo, two AI coding subscriptions at $200/month. He outputs more than the old crew of PM + 5 devs + designer + analyst — and twice as fast. His edge? Nobody told him "you can't."

And no, this isn't just a startup thing. Virtually every business has constraints that AI agents can unlock right now. Most people either haven't figured it out yet or are too afraid to try. The "enterprise is different" objection is increasingly just inertia.

The leverage that used to come from hiring now comes from tools. But tools are cheap. Knowing where to point them is not.

If you're a PM today, your most valuable skill isn't managing sprints or writing tickets. It's making the right call on what to build — fast, and under uncertainty.
