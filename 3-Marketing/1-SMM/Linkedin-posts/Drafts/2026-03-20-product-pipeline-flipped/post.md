# Product Pipeline Flipped

I built a product, integrated Stripe, and sold out the first cohort — all before my developer knew I was doing it.

Here's how: AI research for segments, jobs, competitors, and risks took me 1.5 hours. Building the MVP took a couple more. Stripe integration — 3 hours. The next morning I was selling. First cohort sold out in 24 hours. Second cohort — same.

No dev team. No sprint planning. No Jira tickets. Just a $200/month Claude Code subscription doing what used to require 5-10 engineers.

This changes everything about how products get built.

For the past decade, the product pipeline looked like this: deep CustDev → extensive research → build. And it was rational. When a development mistake cost 10-100x more than a research mistake, you invested heavily in cheap research to de-risk expensive building. Every PM textbook taught this. Every accelerator preached it.

But the economics just flipped.

When code costs essentially nothing to produce, it's now cheaper to build an MVP than to run a full CustDev cycle. The math that justified months of upfront research simply doesn't hold anymore.

So the new pipeline looks radically different: quick AI-powered research (segments, jobs-to-be-done, competitors, positioning, key risks) in 1-2 hours → working MVP in a few more hours → go sell it to a real person. The CustDev interview doesn't disappear — it transforms from a research conversation into a sales conversation.

You're no longer showing up with a slide deck asking "would you use this?" You're showing up with a working product asking "will you buy this?" That's a fundamentally different signal. Declared preferences (what people say they want) are notoriously unreliable. Purchase behavior (what people actually pay for) tells you the truth in minutes.

Yes, AI-generated research carries risks. Segments might be off. Jobs might be partially hallucinated. But here's what changes the calculus: when each iteration costs hours instead of months, you can afford to be wrong more often. Ten fast iterations with a live market will always beat one perfectly researched launch that took six months to ship.

Each cycle — build, sell, learn, rebuild — takes days, not quarters. By your third iteration, you've collected more real market signal than most teams gather in a year of interviews and surveys.

The old pipeline was smart for its era. The new one is smart for ours. The question isn't whether your research is perfect — it's whether you're learning faster than the market is moving.
