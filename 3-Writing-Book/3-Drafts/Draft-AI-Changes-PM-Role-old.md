# The PM role isn't dying -- it's splitting in two

Two facts that shouldn't coexist, but do.

Fact one: in 2025, tech companies cut over [54,000 workers in AI-related layoffs](https://nationalcioreview.com/articles-insights/extra-bytes/ai-forces-over-50000-layoffs-in-2025-at-leading-technology-firms/), with product and program management among the hardest-hit functions. Fact two: AI product management roles now command a [35% salary premium](https://agentstoday.substack.com/p/agents-today-16-the-great-reshuffling), and demand is climbing.

The PM role isn't shrinking. It isn't growing. It's *bifurcating* -- splitting into two trajectories so different they barely share a job title. One leads to obsolescence. The other leads to the most leveraged version of product work that has ever existed.

To understand which track you're on, you need to see what AI is actually repricing -- and it's not what most people think.

## It's not about code -- it's about judgment

A [Perspective AI study](https://getperspective.ai/published/page/6851e02fc1043be42b224395) found that 67% of product managers report anxiety about AI replacing core parts of their role. That anxiety isn't paranoia. It's the right instinct -- aimed at the wrong target.

The threat isn't that AI will take your job. It's that AI is repricing which parts of the job matter. Consider Boris Cherny, head of Claude Code at Anthropic: he [hasn't written a single line of code since November 2025](https://fortune.com/2026/01/29/100-percent-of-code-at-anthropic-and-openai-is-now-ai-written-boris-cherny-roon/), shipping 22-27 pull requests daily -- all AI-generated, with a 200% productivity increase. Yet he's more valuable than ever. Why? Because he still prompts, talks to customers, coordinates across teams, and -- critically -- decides what to build next.

As a16z put it in their [2026 AI apps review](https://a16z.com/notes-on-ai-apps-in-2026/): the hard problem has shifted from "how to build" to "what to build." Marc Andreessen called the current moment a "Mexican standoff between PM, eng, and design -- they're all essentially correct" that they can do each other's work now. The old T-shaped skillset is giving way to E-shaped and F-shaped profiles where you go deep in multiple domains, not just one.

Here's a useful way to think about it. The Risk Assumption Test formula says: Risk Priority = (Probability x Consequences) / Cost of Testing. AI is crushing the denominator -- the cost of testing approaches nearly zero. When testing is cheap, what dominates is the numerator: your judgment about which risks matter, which segments to pursue, where in the value chain the real problem sits. That causal chain -- from market to segment to job-to-be-done to value proposition to unit economics -- is the business judgment that no agent can replicate.

But a dose of honesty: a [METR study](https://metr.org/blog/2025-07-10-early-2025-ai-experienced-os-dev-study/) found that experienced open-source developers were actually 19% *slower* using AI tools, even though they *perceived* they were 20% faster. AI isn't magic. Self-awareness about where it helps -- and where it doesn't -- is itself a judgment skill. Execution-level PM work is being automated; the PMs who thrive are those rising to strategy-level thinking.

If judgment is the new premium skill, the next question is structural: what does a judgment-first PM actually *do* all day?

## From roadmaps to hypothesis pipelines

The short answer: you become an operator of a hypothesis pipeline running at 10x the velocity you're used to.

Shreyas Doshi [has observed](https://maven.com/shreyas-doshi/product-management-career) that 3-4 person AI-augmented startups now deliver the impact of teams of 30-50. The reason isn't just faster coding. It's that the entire product development loop -- hypothesize, build, test, learn -- has compressed. Your feature backlog becomes a hypothesis backlog, ranked by risk priority. PRDs evolve into what Aparna Chennapragada calls ["prompt sets -- the new PRDs"](https://aparnacd.substack.com/p/prompt-sets-are-the-new-prds): artifacts that are simultaneously specification and training data for AI agents.

This shift goes well beyond code generation. Brian Chesky [revealed in February 2026](https://fortune.com/2026/02/17/airbnb-ceo-brian-chesky-says-ai-best-thing-ever-happened-company-warns-other-founders-get-onboard-or-else/) that AI now handles a third of Airbnb's customer service in North America. [76% of product leaders](https://www.mindtheproduct.com/the-2026-ai-product-strategy-huide-how-to-plan-budget-and-build-without-buying-into-the-hype/) are increasing AI investment in 2026. At Shopify, CEO Tobi Lutke [told teams](https://techcrunch.com/2025/04/07/shopify-ceo-tells-teams-to-consider-using-ai-before-growing-headcount/) to demonstrate why AI *can't* do the work before requesting headcount -- and made AI usage part of performance reviews. Within 12-24 months, expect AI to cover not just coding but project management, deployment, and customer operations across the full product cycle.

But here's the honest caveat. As Teresa Torres [pointed out](https://www.producttalk.org/ai-evals-discovery-all-things-product-podcast-with-teresa-torres-petra-wille/), AI accelerates failures too. You can prototype the wrong hypothesis at 10x speed. The bottleneck was never build velocity -- it was always hypothesis quality. Agents can't do strategy, tradeoff prioritization, or ethical judgment. That 90% of resources historically invested in features that never move the needle? AI makes that waste faster, not smaller -- unless your judgment about *what* to test improves alongside your speed.

This new operating model is powerful -- but it's not arriving evenly.

## The fork in the road

The K-shaped bifurcation is already underway. AI PMs command that [35% salary premium](https://agentstoday.substack.com/p/agents-today-16-the-great-reshuffling), yet [75% of employers](https://agentstoday.substack.com/p/agents-today-16-the-great-reshuffling) report they can't find qualified AI-ready product managers. Demand surges at both poles -- strategic AI-augmented PMs and entry-level builders -- while the execution-heavy middle hollows out. As Marc Andreessen frames it: "task loss, not job loss." The tasks disappear, but the job persists -- transformed beyond recognition. LinkedIn [replaced its legendary APM program](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/why-linkedin-is-replacing-pms) with "Associate Product Builder," a title that signals exactly where the profession is heading.

So why aren't more PMs making the shift? Four forces shape every behavior change: the *pull* of AI's value and the *push* of current frustrations compete against the *anxiety* of the unknown and the sheer *gravity* of habit. [HBR research](https://hbr.org/2025/11/overcoming-the-organizational-barriers-to-ai-adoption) shows that hesitation is grounded in ambiguity, not ego. And people overestimate their ability to change habits by roughly 3x.

The answer isn't "adapt or die" -- that framing creates paralysis. The answer is: create safety to experiment. That psychological safety -- where it's okay to fail with new tools, where 80% of experiments won't work and that's normal -- is what separates the upper arm of the K from the lower.

So where does that leave you -- specifically, this Monday morning?

## Your move

Remember those two facts that shouldn't coexist? Layoffs and premium salaries. Contraction and expansion. They coexist because we're watching a role split in real time. The PMs being laid off and the PMs commanding 35% premiums aren't in different industries -- they're often in the same company. The difference isn't talent. It's exposure. The PMs who are thriving didn't start smarter. They started experimenting sooner, in safer conditions.

You don't need a three-year plan. You need a three-week experiment. Pick one hypothesis from your backlog and build a prototype with an AI tool -- not to ship, but to feel how your judgment shifts when testing costs nearly nothing. Replace one PRD with a prompt set. Audit your backlog and ask: which of these are assumptions I could test this week instead of next quarter?

That's the muscle that matters now. Start building it before someone builds it for you.
