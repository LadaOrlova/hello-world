## 2. Key Competitors

- **Status**: Market leader in in-person teen coding camps

  - Day camps: $949--$1,449/week (varies by location)

  - 2-week academies: $4,599--$5,299
  - Payment plans available from $375

## 3. Vibe Coding / AI-Assisted Coding for Teens: THE NEW CATEGORY

### What Is Vibe Coding?
- Term coined by AI researcher **Andrej Karpathy in February 2025**
- Named **Collins English Dictionary Word of the Year 2025**
- Concept: Describe what you want in plain English, and AI builds the code for you
- Global market: **$4.7B** in 2025

### Who Is Teaching Vibe Coding to Teens?

This is a **nascent category with very few dedicated programs**. Here is every program found:

| Program | Ages | Tools Taught | Duration | Notable |
|---------|------|-------------|----------|---------|
| **Modern Age Coders -- Vibe Coding for Teens** | 13--17 | Cursor IDE, GitHub Copilot, v0.dev, Bolt.new, Lovable | 12 weeks | 12+ deployed projects; MERN stack + AI tools; capstone on AWS |
| **Create & Learn -- Advanced Python: Vibe Coding with AI** | Grades 6--12 | ChatGPT, OpenAI APIs | Ongoing | Part of established K-12 platform; small groups of 3-5 |
| **Girls Who Code -- Vibe Coding for Students** | Teens | Replit, Cursor | Ongoing | Free nonprofit; emphasizes equity and access |
| **imagi -- Hour of AI: Vibe Coding** | Grades 6--12 | AI prompts (unspecified) | 1 hour | Free lesson with ready-to-teach slides |
| **Vibe Coding from Scratch (Udemy)** | All ages | Cursor, Windsurf, v0, Bolt | 1h 51m | $54.99; self-paced; not teen-specific |
| **Vibe Coding 101 with Replit (DeepLearning.AI)** | All ages | Replit agents | 1.5 hours | Free; not teen-specific |

### Critical Finding: Almost Nobody Is Teaching Claude Code to Teens

- **Claude Code** is mentioned only by Modern Age Coders (in their description of "terminal-based agentic engineering workflows")
- **Cursor** is the most commonly featured tool across teen programs (Girls Who Code, Modern Age Coders, vibe coding courses)
- **GitHub Copilot** is taught by Modern Age Coders (free for students)
- **ChatGPT** is the most commonly integrated AI tool across all programs (Create & Learn, NextGen, Outschool)
- No program was found that specifically brands itself around teaching teens to use **Claude, Claude Code, or Anthropic products** as the primary curriculum

### This Represents a Clear Market Gap

The "Vibe Coding for Teens" category barely exists. Most established players (iD Tech, Code Ninjas, Codecademy, FunTech) have NOT adapted their curriculum to include vibe coding tools. The few programs that exist are either:
1. Small startups (Modern Age Coders)
2. One course within a larger platform (Create & Learn)
3. Nonprofit initiatives (Girls Who Code)
4. Generic adult courses repurposed for younger audiences (Udemy)

## 4. What Parents Are Saying

### The Big Question: "Should My Kid Still Learn to Code?"

This is the #1 question parents are asking in 2025-2026, appearing across Reddit, LinkedIn, parenting blogs, and search trends. The consensus from experts:

> "Teaching your child to code in 2026 is not about making them a software engineer -- it's about giving them a framework for thinking that will make them better at everything else they do."
-- [Kodely Blog](https://blog.kodely.io/is-coding-still-worth-learning-2026/)

> "A child who learns to code well will use AI to build 10x faster."
-- Multiple sources

### Key Data Points from Surveys

**Pew Research Center (Feb 2026, survey conducted Sept-Oct 2025)**:
- **64% of teens** use AI chatbots; ~30% use them daily
- Only **51% of parents** believe their teen uses chatbots (13-point perception gap)
- **80% of parents** are OK with teens using chatbots for information search
- ~**60% of parents** are OK with chatbots for schoolwork
- **40% of parents** have never discussed chatbots with their teen
- **15% of teens** use AI tools to write code
- **12% of teens** use AI for emotional support
- Source: [Pew Research](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2026/02/24/what-parents-say-about-their-teens-ai-use/)

**National Parents Union (Feb 2026)**:
- **79%** of parents want parental permission required before minors use AI tools
- **86%** want pop-up warnings for sensitive content
- **85%** want parental alerts if children discuss harmful topics
- **52%** see equal benefits and downsides to school AI tools
- **47%** say schools haven't shared their AI policies
- **57%** weren't asked for input on school AI decisions
- Source: [EdWeek](https://www.edweek.org/technology/how-do-parents-want-schools-to-handle-ai-insights-from-a-new-survey/2026/03)

**APA (June 2025)**:
- Health advisory on AI companion software warns it "may displace or interfere with the development of healthy real-world relationships"
- **7 in 10 teenagers** are using AI to help complete homework
- Source: [CRPE](https://crpe.org/ai-is-moving-fast-but-school-responses-and-parent-opinions-are-not/)

**USC (2025)**:
- Nearly **two-thirds of parents** believe AI could undermine children's basic skills
- Source: [Capita](https://capita.org/parents-fear-ai-but-the-risk-to-families-is-not-evenly-distributed/)

### Parent Fears (Ranked by Prevalence)

1. **AI will undermine critical thinking and basic skills** -- 65% of parents
2. **Cheating on homework** -- top concern across forums and surveys
3. **Loss of social skills / too much screen time** -- especially among mothers
4. **Data privacy** -- 39% of mothers don't know if their children's data is collected; only 20% understand AI privacy risks
5. **Lack of guardrails** -- 80%+ want more restrictions
6. **Economic inequality in access** -- higher-income families disproportionately exposed to AI
7. **Emotional dependency on AI chatbots** -- 12% of teens already using AI for emotional support

### What Parents WANT Their Kids to Learn

1. **How to use AI responsibly and ethically** -- Outschool classes on this topic are growing
2. **Critical thinking and problem-solving** -- the "meta-skill" behind coding
3. **Practical AI literacy** -- understanding what AI can and cannot do
4. **Traditional coding fundamentals** -- as a foundation to guide/audit AI output
5. **How to be an "AI-assisted developer"** -- the new career path
6. **Data science and cybersecurity** -- growing parent interest

### Declining Trust Trend

- 2024: 64% of parents supported AI for test prep; 65% for tutoring
- 2025: Dropped to 54% and 60% respectively
- Source: [The Hill](https://thehill.com/homenews/education/5475742-ai-in-schools-parents-poll/)

## 6. Trends Since June 2025

### 1. Vibe Coding Went Mainstream (But Not Yet in Teen Education)

- Andrej Karpathy coined "vibe coding" in February 2025
- Collins Dictionary named it Word of the Year 2025
- Adult courses exploded on Udemy, Coursera, and standalone platforms
- **But for teens**: Only a handful of dedicated programs exist (see Section 3)

### 2. Government Push for AI Education

- **April 2025**: President Trump signed executive order "Advancing Artificial Intelligence Education for American Youth"
- White House Task Force on AI Education established
- Presidential AI Challenge for youth planned
- **141 companies** have pledged to invest in the initiative (as of Sep 2025)
- Source: [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/04/advancing-artificial-intelligence-education-for-american-youth/)

### 3. Juni Learning Collapsed

- September 2025: Mass layoff of 121 employees, WARN Act investigation
- Created a gap in the premium 1:1 online coding tutoring market for kids
- Source: [Strauss Borrelli](https://straussborrelli.com/2025/09/08/juni-learning-warn-act-investigation/)

### 4. Parent Trust in AI Is Declining

- Support for AI in schools dropped ~10 percentage points from 2024 to 2025
- Parents want MORE guardrails, not fewer
- But 43%+ recognize AI will be beneficial for their children's future

### 5. Teen AI Usage Is Exploding

- 64% of teens use chatbots (up significantly); 30% daily
- 70% using AI for homework
- 15% using AI to write code
- Parents are significantly underestimating their teens' usage (13-point gap)

### 6. "Should Kids Learn to Code?" Became the Defining Debate

- TIME Magazine, Pew Research, Brookings, CNN -- all covering this question
- Expert consensus: YES, but the WHY has shifted from "to become developers" to "to think computationally and supervise AI"
- Demand for digital skills grew **35% in the past two years**

### 7. European AI Literacy Push

- EU Code Week 2025: 10M young Europeans participated
- 2026 AI Literacy Grants launching for teachers in 5 EU countries
- Franchise models (Logiscool, Algorithmics) expanding across Europe
- Source: [EU Code Week](https://codeweek.eu/blog/2025-2026-roadmap/)

## 7. Strategic Implications

### The Market Gap

There is a clear, uncontested gap at the intersection of:
- **Teen audience** (13--17 years old)
- **Vibe coding / AI-assisted development** as the core skill
- **Professional AI tools** (Claude Code, Cursor, Copilot) as the toolkit
- **Guided, live instruction** (not self-paced Udemy courses)

No major player currently occupies this position. The closest competitors are:
- Modern Age Coders (small, online, 12-week program)
- Create & Learn (one course within a broader catalog)
- Girls Who Code (nonprofit, introductory level)

### Specifically: Nobody Teaches Claude Code to Teens

Among all competitors researched, **not a single program** centers its curriculum around Claude or Anthropic products. This is a first-mover opportunity.

### Parent Messaging Insight

Marketing to parents must address:
1. "This teaches your kid to SUPERVISE AI, not be replaced by it"
2. "Coding is the new literacy -- AI makes it MORE important, not less"
3. "Your teen is already using AI (64% are). This teaches them to use it professionally"
4. "Small groups, live mentors, real projects -- not passive screen time"
5. Address the trust gap with transparency about AI guardrails and ethical use

### Pricing Positioning

- Below iD Tech residential ($4,599--$5,299) but above Outschool marketplace ($10--$30/session)
- The $500--$2,000 range for a multi-week online program appears underserved
- Subscription model ($200--$500/month) could work for ongoing vibe coding instruction
