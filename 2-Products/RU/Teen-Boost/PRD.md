## 1. Product Overview

**Name:** Teen Boost

**One-liner:** The first vibe coding bootcamp for teenagers (13-17). No syntax — they talk to AI and build real apps in one week.

**What it is:** A one-week intensive where teens aged 13-17 learn to build real software products by talking to AI. No prior coding experience required. Students go from zero to a deployed, working application in 7 days — not by memorizing syntax, but by learning to collaborate with AI as a development partner.

**Based on:** The proven Boost intensive for adults — 500+ graduates across 4 cohorts, 9.2/10 NPS, 70%+ completion rate to Demo Day. Adult Boost has validated the core format: workshop-based learning, vibe coding methodology, and the progression from idea to deployed MVP in one week. Teen Boost adapts this proven model with age-appropriate pacing, projects, and support structures.

**Two markets:**
- **Primary:** US / Western Europe (English) — larger TAM, higher willingness-to-pay, first-mover advantage in an uncontested category
- **Secondary:** Russia (Russian) — existing brand equity from adult Boost, established infrastructure, operational familiarity

**Core belief:** Traditional coding education for teens (syntax-first, years-long progression) is being made obsolete by AI. The teens who learn to work *with* AI now will have a 5-10 year head start over those learning to write code manually. Teen Boost is how they start.

## 3. Competitive Analysis

### Direct Competitors: None

There are **no direct competitors** offering "vibe coding for teens" — a bootcamp where teenagers build real applications by talking to AI without learning traditional programming syntax. This is a new category.

### Traditional Coding Camps (Indirect Competitors)

| Competitor | Price | Format | What They Teach | Key Weakness vs. Teen Boost |
|-----------|-------|--------|----------------|----------------------------|
| **iD Tech** | $749-$1,299/week | In-person camps | Python, Java, game dev, robotics | Traditional syntax-first approach; teens spend weeks before building anything real |
| **AI Camp** | $749+ | Online/in-person | AI/ML concepts, Python | Teaches AI theory and traditional coding, not AI-native development |
| **Code Ninjas** | $199-$299/mo | Drop-in centers | Game-based coding curriculum | Gamified but slow progression; no real-world projects |
| **Codecademy (Teen)** | $20-$40/mo | Self-paced online | Syntax drills, courses | No live instruction, low completion rates, no portfolio outcome |
| **Juni Learning** | $250-$350/mo | 1-on-1 tutoring | Python, Java, web dev | Expensive per hour, traditional approach, no AI integration |

### AI-Adjacent Education (Indirect)

| Competitor | Price | What They Teach | Key Weakness vs. Teen Boost |
|-----------|-------|----------------|----------------------------|
| **Outschool** | $10-$50/class | Various AI intro classes | One-off classes, no cohort progression, inconsistent quality |
| **Create & Learn** | $20-$60/class | AI concepts, Scratch + AI | Younger audience focus, AI as topic not tool |
| **AI4ALL** | Free/scholarship | AI ethics and concepts | Academic/nonprofit focus, not building real products |
| **Google CS First** | Free | Scratch-based coding | No AI, basic visual programming, no real deployments |

### Competitive Advantages

1. **Category creation:** "Vibe coding for teens" does not exist yet. First mover defines the space.
2. **Speed to outcome:** Working deployed app in 7 days vs. months of syntax learning before building anything.
3. **AI-native methodology:** AI is the tool, not the topic. Teens learn to work WITH AI, not just learn ABOUT AI.
4. **Proven adult model:** Adapted from 500+ graduate program, not built from scratch.
5. **Zero barrier to entry:** No prior coding knowledge, no prerequisites, no placement tests.
6. **Tangible portfolio:** Every student leaves with a live, deployed application they can show.

### Competitive Moat (Why This Is Hard to Copy)

- The vibe coding methodology is proprietary and refined over 4 adult cohorts
- Curriculum requires deep expertise in both AI tools and pedagogy
- Network effects: student showcase creates aspirational content for next cohort
- Speed advantage: competitors would need 6-12 months to develop and validate a similar program

## 4. Positioning

### Primary Positioning

**"The first bootcamp where teens build real apps by talking to AI."**

**Why each word matters:**
- **"First"** — Category creation. No competition to compare against. Sets the reference point.
- **"Bootcamp"** — Intensive, structured, serious. Not a toy or game. Parents respect the format.
- **"Teens"** — Age-specific. Not a generic course diluted for all ages. Designed for 13-17.
- **"Build"** — Active verb. Creation, not consumption. Addresses "productive screen time" desire.
- **"Real apps"** — Tangible outcome. Not "learn concepts" or "complete exercises." Something you can touch, use, show.
- **"Talking to AI"** — Zero barrier. Parents understand this instantly. No scary "learn to code" framing.

### Alternative Positions (Considered, May Use in Specific Channels)

| Position | Best For | Risk |
|----------|----------|------|
| "The coding camp for a world where AI writes code" | Early adopters, tech-savvy parents, LinkedIn | More confrontational; may alienate traditional coding parents |
| "Give your teen a 5-year head start in AI" | Future-proof parents (Segment A) | Sounds like hype if not backed by proof |
| "From screen time to build time" | Productive screen time parents (Segment D) | Negative framing ("your kid wastes time") can backfire |
| "Your teen's first startup — built in one week" | Ambitious parents, entrepreneurial teens | "Startup" can sound frivolous for teens |

### Messaging Framework

**For parents (primary buyer):**
- Headline: "Your teen will build a real app in one week — by talking to AI."
- Subhead: "No coding experience needed. No syntax to memorize. Just ideas, AI, and a week of guided building."
- Proof: "Based on our adult bootcamp with 500+ graduates and 9.2/10 satisfaction."
- CTA: "Enroll for the next cohort" / "See what past students built"

**For teens (influencer / advocate):**
- Headline: "Build your own app this week. No cap."
- Subhead: "You already talk to ChatGPT. Now learn to build real stuff with AI."
- Proof: Gallery of teen-built projects (once available; adult projects initially)
- CTA: "Show this to your parents" / "See what you can build"

## 6. Product Design

### 6.1 Program Structure

The program follows the proven adult Boost structure, adapted for teen attention spans, interests, and skill levels.

**Duration:** 7 days (one workshop per day)
**Time per workshop:** 2 hours live + 1-2 hours of self-paced work
**Cohort size:** 25-30 students
**Curator ratio:** 1 curator per 5-6 students

| Day | Workshop | Topic | Outcome |
|-----|----------|-------|---------|
| 0 (Pre-course) | Setup Workshop | Install VS Code, Claude Code, accounts. Parent onboarding. | All tools working, parents informed |
| 1 | Idea + PRD | Choose a project idea. Write a Product Requirements Document with AI. | PRD complete, project scope defined |
| 2 | Landing Page | Build a landing page for their project using AI. Deploy it live. | Live website anyone can visit |
| 3 | MVP Build | Build the core functionality of their app with AI. | Working application code |
| 4 | Deploy + Data | Deploy MVP to a server. Learn to work with data and APIs. | Live, accessible application |
| 5 | Polish + Features | Add features, fix bugs, improve design. Automation intro. | Polished, feature-complete app |
| 6 | Demo Day | Present projects to peers, parents, and judges. Celebrate. | Portfolio piece, presentation skills |

### 6.2 Key Adaptations from Adult Boost

| Aspect | Adult Boost | Teen Boost | Why |
|--------|------------|------------|-----|
| Workshop duration | 3-4 hours | 2 hours live + async | Teen attention spans; school schedules |
| Project types | Business MVPs, SaaS tools | Personal apps, games, social tools, school projects | Relevance and motivation |
| Support ratio | 1:12 | 1:5-6 | Teens need more hands-on help |
| Parent involvement | None | Pre-course onboarding, Demo Day invitation, progress updates | Parents are buyers, need visibility |
| Technical setup | Self-guided | Dedicated setup workshop (Day 0) + tech support | Teens have less technical confidence |
| Tone | Professional | Encouraging, peer-oriented, meme-friendly | Age-appropriate engagement |
| Demo Day | Optional showcase | Structured event with parents invited | Creates shareable moment, justifies purchase |

### 6.3 Example Teen Projects (Aspiration Gallery)

Projects should feel personally relevant to teens:
- A study buddy app that quizzes you on any subject
- A social app that helps friend groups decide where to eat
- A personal portfolio/blog website
- A game recommendation engine based on your preferences
- A mood tracker with AI-generated journal prompts
- A school club management tool
- A workout/fitness tracker with AI coaching
- A meme generator powered by AI
- A budget tracker for teens' allowance and spending

### 6.4 Curriculum Principles

1. **Show, don't lecture.** Every concept is demonstrated by building something, not by slides.
2. **Ship daily.** Every workshop ends with something deployed or visible. Momentum over perfection.
3. **Peer energy.** Teens learn from and are motivated by each other. Showcase peer work frequently.
4. **Safe to fail.** AI makes it easy to try, break, and rebuild. Normalize experimentation.
5. **Parent-visible outcomes.** Parents should be able to see progress at every stage.

## 11. Legal & Compliance

### US Market (COPPA Compliance)

- **Parental consent** required before collecting any data from children under 13. For 13-17, parental consent required for enrollment and payment.
- **Data minimization:** Collect only necessary data (name, email, age, parent contact).
- **No direct marketing to minors** without parental consent.
- **Privacy policy** clearly describes data practices in plain language.
- **Data retention:** Student data deleted 12 months after course completion unless parent opts in to alumni communications.

### Content Safety

- All AI-generated content reviewed by curators during workshops
- Content moderation guidelines for student projects
- Incident escalation protocol for inappropriate content
- Parent notification process for any safety concerns

### Terms of Service

- Parent/guardian must agree to ToS on behalf of minor
- Refund policy clearly stated (full refund through Day 2)
- Intellectual property: students own their projects
- Liability limitations for AI-generated content

## 13. Success Criteria for v1.0

The product is considered validated and ready to scale when:

1. **NPS >= 8.5/10** from the first 2 pilot cohorts
2. **Completion rate >= 70%** (Workshop 1 through Demo Day)
3. **80%+ of students** deploy a working project
4. **80%+ of parents** say they would recommend to another family
5. **Refund rate < 10%** during pilot phase
6. **Unit economics positive:** Revenue per cohort > cost per cohort (curators + tools + infrastructure)

## 14. Open Questions

1. **Discord vs. Telegram for teen cohort communication?** Discord is more natural for US/EU teens but requires age verification (13+). Telegram is simpler but less engaging for teens.
2. **Should we offer a "recordings only" tier?** Adult Boost has this ($29,900 vs. $39,900 RUB). For teens, live interaction may be critical — async might not work for this age group.
3. **How to handle timezones for international cohorts?** US East, US West, and EU timezones may need separate cohorts.
4. **Should teens use their own Claude Code accounts or shared/managed accounts?** Own accounts teach responsibility but add setup friction and cost. Managed accounts simplify operations.
5. **What is the minimum age we support?** 13 is the COPPA threshold, but maturity varies. Should we require a brief intake/readiness assessment?
6. **Advanced course for graduates?** What does "Teen Boost 2.0" look like? Deeper AI projects, multi-week format, specialization tracks (game dev, social apps, data projects)?
