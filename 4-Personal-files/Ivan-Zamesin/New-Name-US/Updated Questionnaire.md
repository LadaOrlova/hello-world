## 1. What are we naming?

**We need names for two things (which may or may not share a single name):**

### A. The Methodology (currently called "Aura" internally)

An integral meta-framework that synchronizes all company functions (product, marketing, sales, support) around a single strategy. It integrates four core components:

- **Advanced Jobs To Be Done (AJTBD):** Understanding what customers actually need (their "jobs") rather than demographics. Step-by-step algorithms for creating value, not just theory.
- **Unit Economics:** Every strategic decision is grounded in financial modeling — does the math work?
- **Risk Assumption Test (RAT):** Prioritizing the riskiest hypotheses first — test what matters most before investing.
- **ABCDX Segmentation:** Classifying customer segments by profitability and satisfaction.

**Core attributes of the methodology:**
* Brings clarity and decisiveness
* Provides strategic market vision and helps pick the most effective strategy at any point in time
* Prevents ~100 common founder/product/exec mistakes that lead to stillborn launches, slow growth, vulnerability to competitor innovation
* Works across all levels — from tactics (landing copy, button naming, feature activation) to strategy (market/segment choice, unique value creation and communication, strategic advantages, escaping direct competition, positioning)
* Validated by 12,000+ alumni: not just theory, but a practice with real-world evidence
* It's a "coherent operating system" for product decisions, not a collection of separate tools
* You don't have to throw everything out and start over. Compatible with North Star Framework, HEART, OKR, BMC/VPC
* The fundamental insight: the problem in most companies is **desynchronization** — research team does studies that product ignores, product ships features marketing can't sell, marketing attracts the wrong leads. This methodology synchronizes everything.

### B. The Product 

An AI-powered service that helps founders and product managers figure out **what to do next** in order to get maximum profit — the most effective next step for maximum business impact. It's a hybrid of a course and a tool: it simultaneously teaches product thinking and generates personalized documents.

**What the product does:**
1. **Teaches product thinking** — asks questions, explains "why", builds the skill of systematic product reasoning (based on the methodology above)
2. **Generates documents** — PRD, risk assessment, landing page copy, analytics plan — personalized to the user's specific context and idea
3. **Teaches vibe-coding** — practical micro-guides on how to take these documents and build the product yourself using Cursor, Claude Code, Lovable

**Key differentiator:** The product does NOT generate code and does NOT deploy. Output = documents + knowledge + skills. The user builds the product themselves using external AI tools.

**Target audience for the product:**
- Non-technical founders (0→1): have an idea, need to understand what to build and validate before investing $5–20K
- Solopreneurs / side-hustlers: want to quickly assess if an idea is worth their limited evening hours
- AI-curious professionals (PMs, marketers, designers): want a product thinking framework + practical AI experience

**Value:** $199/month replaces a $150–500/hour consultant. 2–3 hours instead of 20–40 hours on courses.

## 3. Who are your competitors?

### Methodology competitors:
* The main practical competitor is "building on intuition": a founder/PM shipping ideas without a system, which leads to predictable mistakes.
* Classic Jobs To Be Done: useful but narrow and requires do-it-yourself adaptation. Our framework is broader and more practical: it includes unit economics, hypothesis ranking, and algorithms for finding non-obvious solutions. It's the *applied* version of JTBD with clear how-to.
* Other frameworks (e.g., IDEO's Design Thinking): partial, often expensive, and not an operating system.
* Business Model Canvas by Strategyzer: operates at our level but doesn't provide algorithms and strategies.
* In reality, the strongest competitor is the habit of "shipping features" based on hunches, copying, or scattered insights.

### Product competitors:
* ChatPRD ($15/month): generates PRDs but doesn't teach "why" and has no methodology behind it
* DimeADozen ($45/report): one-time report, no learning, no framework
* LeanPivot.ai ($5–99/month): 46+ tools, overloaded, shallow
* Buildcamp ($400/year): video courses on vibe-coding, no product thinking
* Generic ChatGPT/Claude usage: no methodology, no personalization, no step-by-step guidance
* Human consultants ($150–500/hour): limited availability, not async

* "Coherent OS" vs. "tool collection": existing frameworks are modules, parts (metrics, prioritization, discovery, design); ours is a cohesive system where everything connects end-to-end.

* **NEW — the product differentiation:** The only product on the market that combines (1) product thinking education, (2) AI document generation personalized to user's context, and (3) vibe-coding learning. No one does all three.
