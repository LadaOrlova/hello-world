## Context

Vanya is going on vacation in May. The long-term goal is to remove the founder from BOOST operations entirely. During the call with Sasha (April 8), four options emerged. Two were selected for detailed analysis: Concept 2 and Concept 4.

**Important:** in both options, a live curator remains. The only difference is who delivers the intro part and how much the curator takes on the trainer role.

---

## Concept 2 — Media Trainer Instead of Vanya

### Core idea
A new host appears at workshops — someone with media presence, technical competence, and charisma. Leads the program instead of Vanya, the workshop format stays the same. Vanya remains in the brand, but not in operations.

### What it looks like for the student
You come to BOOST — not Vanya, but someone "from Zamesin Academy": an expert with a clear background. Same program, methodology, quality.

### What needs to be done
- Find a candidate: vibe-coder with teaching experience, media presence, and charisma (profile — `trainer-requirements.md`)
- Run a test cohort of 20–40 people with the new trainer — end of May
- Measure: NPS, purchase objections, completion rate
- Based on results — decide on a permanent model

### Pros
- Format stays familiar: live host, live energy, interaction
- With the right person — minimal loss of value for the student
- Removes Vanya from operations entirely
- Price for students can be maintained

### Risks and challenges
- **Brand risk**: part of the audience comes for Vanya personally — possible cognitive resistance. This risk is also present in C4: even in recordings, "recorded Vanya instead of live Vanya" is a change students will feel
- **Rare profile**: vibe-coding + charisma + pedagogy + product thinking all at once — hard to find
- **Dependency shifts, not disappears**: we don't eliminate dependency on a personality, we just swap Vanya for a trainer
- **Higher trainer CAC**: margin decreases — see Economics section

### Curator requirements in C2
Unchanged from current model. Curator supports students, answers questions, runs breakout rooms.

## Concept 4 — Vanya's Recording (intro part) + Curator as Trainer

### Core idea
Vanya on recording — only the intro conceptual part of each workshop: context, market transformation, methodological frame. Then the curator takes over live: practice, assignment review, group work. The curator becomes the full trainer of the intensive.

### What it looks like for the student
Workshop starts with a short Vanya video — he explains the essence and sets the direction. Then the curator takes over and leads the practical part: assignments, review, questions, group work. Vanya is present by voice and face, but not in real time.

### Structure of one workshop
```
1. Vanya's recording (15–20 min): context, methodology, what we're doing and why
2. Curator live (60–90 min): practice, assignment review, Q&A
3. Curator wrap-up: what we did, what's next
```

### Pros
- **No additional trainer cost**: recording is a one-time investment
- **Vanya on recording** — present by voice and face, methodological authority preserved
- **Scales without content limits**: recording doesn't get tired
- **Manageability**: ZIS tracks who watched, who fell behind
- **Completion rate unaffected**: format stays live — curator leads practice in real time

### Risks and challenges
- **Brand risk comparable to C2**: "live Vanya vs recorded Vanya" — also a change. Students who came specifically for live Vanya will feel the difference
- **Scalability equally constrained**: in both options the ceiling is the number of available live curator-trainers. Recording scales, but curators are real people
- **Higher curator requirements**: takes on trainer role — see below
- **Requires preparation**: recording/editing, platform (ZIS), curator training — minimum 6–8 weeks
- **Impact on WTP**: removing live Vanya may pressure willingness to pay 39,900 RUB

### What changes in the curator role in C4
In the current model, the curator is support: answers questions, helps in breakout rooms. In C4, **the curator becomes the intensive trainer**: holds a group of 100–150 people, manages dynamics, leads the entire practical section independently.

This requires a different level — closer to the profile in `trainer-requirements.md`:
- Can hold a large audience live
- Technically confident: demonstrates, not just explains
- Understands methodology well enough to answer independently
- Can improvise

**Scalability conclusion:** both options are equally constrained by the availability of suitable live people. The only difference is the cost of those people.

## Economics: What BOOST Costs in Each Concept

### Baseline model (current, with Vanya)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Students per cohort | ~120 |
| "Recordings" tier | 29,900 RUB |
| "Intensive" tier | 39,900 RUB |
| Average check (estimate) | ~36,000 RUB |
| Revenue per cohort | ~4,300,000 RUB |
| Curators | ~10 people, paid per cohort |
| Vanya | Leads workshops as founder — hidden cost of his time |

Vanya doesn't receive a separate fee for workshops — it's his product. But his time has real value: 7 workshops × 3–4 hours = ~25–30 hours per week of founder time.

---

### Concept 2 — Media Trainer

Main new expense — trainer fee. Two contract options:

**Option A — Fixed rate per cohort**

| Trainer level | Rate/cohort | % of revenue |
|---------------|-------------|--------------|
| Strong graduate / niche expert | 100–150K RUB | 2–4% |
| Recognized niche expert | 200–350K RUB | 5–8% |
| Media figure with audience | 400–700K RUB | 10–16% |

**Option B — Revenue share**

At 4.3M RUB revenue:
- 10% = 430K RUB
- 15% = 645K RUB
- 20% = 860K RUB

**Impact on margin (estimate at 120 students):**

| Trainer | Cost | Revenue | Margin minus trainer |
|---------|------|---------|----------------------|
| Strong graduate | 150K | 4,300K | −3.5% |
| Niche expert | 300K | 4,300K | −7% |
| Media figure | 600K | 4,300K | −14% |

**Student price:** keep at 39,900 — live format unchanged, price justification holds.

---

### Concept 4 — Vanya's Recording + Curator-Trainer

One-time investment — filming intro sections (7 workshops × 20 min). Estimate:
- Studio + editing: 50–100K RUB, one-time
- Vanya's time: ~3–5 hours of filming — once, then not needed

Ongoing "trainer" costs: none. But curators take on more responsibility → higher rate.

**Estimated change in curator costs:**

| Option | Curators | Rate/person/cohort (estimate) | Total |
|--------|----------|-------------------------------|-------|
| Current model | 10 | X | 10X |
| C4 (curator-trainer) | 10 | 1.3–1.5X | 13–15X |

Curator cost increase: +30–50% of current rate. No external trainer cost.

**Student price question in C4:**

| Scenario | Price | Logic |
|----------|-------|-------|
| Keep | 39,900 RUB | Vanya on recording — value preserved, format stays live |
| Slightly lower | 34,900 RUB | Acknowledge the change, remove "what am I paying for" objection |
| Remove "Recordings" tier | — | The tier loses its meaning — all students watch the same video |

---

### Economics Comparison

| | C2 — Media Trainer | C4 — Recording + Curator |
|--|-------------------|--------------------------|
| One-time investment | None | 50–100K (filming) |
| Ongoing "trainer" cost | 150–700K/cohort | None |
| Curator cost increase | None | +30–50% of current rate |
| Student price | Maintained (39,900) | Risk of pressure (possibly 34,900) |
| Margin | Decreases by trainer cost | Stable or slightly higher |
| With more cohorts | Trainer cost multiplies | Filming cost is one-time |

**Economics conclusion:** C4 is more profitable at scale — one-time investment vs. recurring cost. C2 is faster to launch, but margin bleeds every cohort.
