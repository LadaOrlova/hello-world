## Thesis 1: Your idea is already dead

### BODY:
90-95% of new products fail. The reason is almost always the same: team was wrong about something fundamental — segment, job, value prop, channel, economics — and discovered it too late. Goal of early stage: buy knowledge about what will kill your idea. We don't launch products — we purchase validated learnings, one assumption at a time. Pivot = surgical act of changing a specific assumption that turned out wrong. Nuance: killing on averages is its own mistake — a foundational cohort can suddenly stick even when average metrics look terrible.

### DATA:
- [ ] T1-D1: 90-95% of new products fail
### CONCEPT:
- [ ] T1-C1: "Buy knowledge about what will kill your idea"
- [ ] T1-C2: "We don't launch products — we purchase validated learnings"
- [ ] T1-C3: Pivot = surgical act of changing a specific wrong assumption
- [ ] T1-C4: Killing on averages ≠ correct; foundational cohort matters
### CASE:
- [ ] T1-CS1: Notion — V1 "programming tool for non-coders" failed, fired 4 employees, moved to Kyoto, rebuilt as modular workspace, $10B valuation — [Figma blog](https://www.figma.com/blog/design-on-a-deadline-how-notion-pulled-itself-back-from-the-brink-of-failure/)

## Thesis 2: Start with the job, not the solution

### BODY:
Jobs are the root cause of everything in your product. Product exists to perform a job for a customer, profit appears only when you create real added value within a specific segment. Most common mistake: teams skip segment and job selection and jump straight to building a solution. Correct sequence: segment → job → value → communication. AI can accelerate hypothesis generation but over-reliance leads to less validation (arXiv).

### DATA:
- [ ] T2-D1: Wispr Flow: 20% free-to-paid conversion vs 3-4% industry average
- [ ] T2-D2: Wispr Flow raised $81M total
### CONCEPT:
- [ ] T2-C1: Correct sequence: segment → job → value → communication
- [ ] T2-C2: "Fall in love with the technology, not the problem" = mistake
### COUNTER:

## Thesis 3: AI made building free ≠ learning free

### BODY:
AI collapsed the build step — prototype in a weekend. But speed of code ≠ speed of learning. "More code, less validation" anti-pattern everywhere now. Right approach: use AI as accelerator of artifacts, not substitute for learning. Measure progress in validated checks, not features shipped.

### DATA:
- [ ] T3-D1: METR study: AI tools can slow experienced developers while they feel faster — [URL](https://metr.org/blog/2025-07-10-early-2025-ai-experienced-os-dev-study/)
- [ ] T3-D2: Vibe-coding = "rolling the dice" (stochastic output) — [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.22418)
### CONCEPT:
- [ ] T3-C1: "More code, less validation" anti-pattern
- [ ] T3-C2: AI = accelerator of artifacts, not substitute for learning
### CASE:
- [ ] T3-CS1: EnrichLead — 100% AI-coded SaaS, collapsed in 72 hours, client-side security — [URL](https://ruinunes.com/vibe-coding-trap-ai-built-mvp/)

## Thesis 4: The killer assumption formula

### BODY:
PMF is a continuum, not binary. At any moment, business has a single most dangerous assumption. Prioritization: (probability wrong × consequences) / cost of testing. Elena Verna: PMF treadmill demands continuous testing loop. Danger: "AI tourists" inflate early metrics. Set signal horizon before seeing data.

### DATA:
- [ ] T4-D1: Popsa: 4x conversion from changing one word in tagline
### QUOTE:
- [ ] T4-Q1: Elena Verna on PMF treadmill — [Lenny's](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/the-new-ai-growth-playbook-for-2026-elena-verna)
### CONCEPT:
- [ ] T4-C1: Killer assumption formula: (probability × consequences) / cost
- [ ] T4-C2: Signal horizon (e.g. Month 3 retention)
- [ ] T4-C3: "AI tourists" inflate early metrics
### CASE:

## Thesis 5: The hypothesis factory OS

### BODY:
AI turned founder into factory operator. Throughput without validation = noise. OS with 4 components: assumption ledger, decision log, staged validation, explicit stop criteria.

### DATA:
- [ ] T5-D1: Spotify: 520 experiments across 58 teams, 12% win rate, 64% learning rate
### CONCEPT:
- [ ] T5-C1: 4-component OS: assumption ledger, decision log, staged validation, stop criteria
- [ ] T5-C2: Learning velocity > experiment velocity
### CASE:
- [ ] T5-CS1: Spotify — Experiments with Learning framework, shifted to learning velocity — [URL](https://engineering.atspotify.com/2025/9/spotifys-experiments-with-learning-framework)

## Thesis 6: PMF treadmill — perpetual engine

### BODY:
PMF becoming less durable in every market. Strategy "find PMF, now optimize and scale" = trap. Solution: two loops simultaneously — Loop 1 (optimize current fit) + Loop 2 (continuous innovation).

### DATA:
- [ ] T6-D1: Jasper AI: $120M ARR, $1.5B valuation → revenue crashed to $35M
### QUOTE:
- [ ] T6-Q1: Elena Verna: "what used to be long-horizon innovation is now a quarterly reality" — [URL](https://www.elenaverna.com/p/the-product-market-fit-treadmill)
### CONCEPT:
- [ ] T6-C1: Two loops: optimize current fit + continuous innovation
- [ ] T6-C2: "PMF is not something you find. It is something you keep finding."
### CASE:
- [ ] T6-CS1: Jasper AI — $120M→$35M ARR, ChatGPT launched, 3 pivots in 12 months — [Contrary Research](https://research.contrary.com/company/jasper)
- [ ] T6-CS2: Lovable — $200M ARR in <1 year, PMF re-earned every 90 days — [Elena Verna](https://www.elenaverna.com/p/6-months-at-lovable-and-why-i-had)
