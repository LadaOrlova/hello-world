## Core idea
PMF is not a one-time event — it's a treadmill you re-earn every 90 days. The playbook: start with jobs/segments, use AI to accelerate artifacts (not substitute validation), prioritize killer assumptions, run a hypothesis factory, and accept the treadmill.

## Theses (from Ivan's current draft — PRESERVE THIS LOGIC)

### Thesis 1: Your idea is already dead — you just don't know what kills it yet
90-95% products fail. The reason: team was wrong about something fundamental and discovered it too late. Goal of early stage: buy knowledge about what will kill your idea. "We don't launch products — we purchase validated learnings." Pivot = surgical act of changing a specific wrong assumption. Notion case: V1 failed as "programming tool for non-coders" → pivoted to modular workspace → $10B. Nuance: killing on averages is its own mistake — foundational cohort can stick even when average metrics look terrible.

### Thesis 2: Start with the job, not the solution
Jobs = root cause of everything. Product exists to perform a job, profit appears when you create real added value within a specific segment. Most common mistake: skip segment/job selection, jump to solution. Correct sequence: segment → job → value → communication. Wispr Flow case: killed 16-year hardware dream, pivoted to macOS dictation app, 20% conversion vs 3-4% industry average, raised $81M. AI can accelerate hypothesis generation but over-reliance leads to less validation (arXiv research).

### Thesis 3: AI made building free. It did not make learning free.
AI collapsed the build step — prototype a working product in a weekend. But speed of code ≠ speed of learning. METR study: AI tools can slow experienced developers while they feel faster. "More code, less validation" anti-pattern. EnrichLead case: 100% AI-coded, collapsed in 72 hours (client-side security). Right approach: use AI as accelerator of artifacts, not substitute for learning. Measure progress in validated checks, not features shipped.

### Thesis 4: The killer assumption formula
PMF is a continuum, not binary. Prioritization: (probability wrong × consequences) / cost of testing. Elena Verna: PMF treadmill demands continuous testing loop. Danger: "AI tourists" inflate early metrics → set signal horizon (e.g. Month 3 retention). Popsa case: changed one word in tagline → 4x conversion. The test cost: one round of user interviews.

### Thesis 5: The hypothesis factory — your operating system
AI turned founder into factory operator. More hypotheses ≠ more knowledge without validation. OS with 4 components: assumption ledger, decision log, staged validation, explicit stop criteria. Spotify case: 520 experiments, 12% win rate, 64% learning rate. Shifted metric from experiment velocity to learning velocity.

### Thesis 6: The PMF treadmill — fit is a perpetual engine
Jasper AI case: $120M ARR, $1.5B valuation → ChatGPT launched → traffic -30%, revenue crashed to $35M, both founders out. PMF becoming less durable in every market. Solution: two loops simultaneously — Loop 1 (optimize current fit) + Loop 2 (continuous innovation). Elena Verna: "what used to be long-horizon innovation is now quarterly reality."
