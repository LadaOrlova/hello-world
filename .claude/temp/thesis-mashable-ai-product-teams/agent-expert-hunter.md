## NEW THESES (extending/challenging the article's core arguments)

### 1. The "Agent Manager" as the New PM
**Expands article thesis:** The PM role doesn't just go "up a level of abstraction" — it splits into two new roles: **product strategist** (decides what to build) and **agent manager** (orchestrates how AI builds it). HBR (Feb 2026) compares agent managers to how PMs became essential during the software revolution. Salesforce already employs them full-time. This reframes the article from "PMs become more important" to "the PM role forks."

### 2. The Polarization Effect: AI Amplifies What You Already Are
**Nuances article thesis:** Laura Tacho's data (via Pragmatic Engineer): some organizations face **2x more incidents** with AI tools, others see **50% fewer**. AI doesn't universally help or harm — it amplifies existing organizational health. Good teams get better; dysfunctional teams get worse faster. This adds a critical "it depends" to the article's claims.

### 3. The Mid-Career Squeeze
**Expands "roles go up a level" thesis:** Both Simon Willison and Gergely Orosz identify mid-career engineers as the most vulnerable cohort — not juniors (who onboard fast with AI), not seniors (who leverage deep experience), but the middle. Cloudflare and Shopify each hired ~1,000 interns because AI cut ramp-up from a month to a week. This creates a specific, vivid human story for the article.

### 4. The Vibe Coding Hangover / Technical Debt Bomb
**Counterargument to "build in a weekend":** Sure, you can build in a weekend. But 45% of AI-generated code fails security tests. Experienced developers are 19% SLOWER with AI tools. 75% of tech leaders expect moderate-to-severe technical debt from AI-speed practices by 2026. The weekend app works... until it doesn't. This gives the article essential balance.

### 5. Karpathy's Evolution: From "Vibe Coding" to "Agentic Engineering"
**Reframes article thesis:** Even the person who coined the term has moved past it. The real shift isn't "anyone can code" — it's "everyone becomes an agent orchestrator." This is a higher-order skill than vibe coding. The article can use Karpathy's own arc as a narrative device: the democratization of coding was chapter 1; chapter 2 is the rise of the orchestrator class.

### 6. The SaaSpocalypse: $1 Trillion Wiped
**Expands "SaaS market shrinks" thesis:** The SaaSpocalypse is no longer theoretical. $1T+ in market cap gone. The per-seat pricing model is existentially threatened when AI agents replace human users. But — the recovery has already begun (April 2026), suggesting the market is repricing, not dying. Companies that pivot to AI-native pricing (outcome-based, agent-based) will survive.
