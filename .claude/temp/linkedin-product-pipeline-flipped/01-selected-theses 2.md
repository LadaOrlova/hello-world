# Selected Theses

## T1: The old pipeline was rational because dev was expensive.
The CustDev → Research → Build sequence existed because errors at the development stage cost 10-100x more than errors at the research stage. Investing in cheap research to de-risk expensive development was economically sound.

## T2: Code becoming free flips the economics.
A $200/mo AI coding subscription now outputs what a team of 5-10 developers used to produce. When code is essentially free, it's cheaper to build an MVP than to run a full CustDev cycle.

## T3: The new pipeline — AI research → MVP → sell.
Quick AI-powered research (segments, jobs, competitors, risks) in 1-2 hours → MVP in a couple hours → go sell to a real person. The CustDev interview transforms from research into a sales conversation.

## T4: AI research is imprecise, but MVP + live feedback compensates.
AI-generated jobs and segments carry risks of inaccuracy. But an MVP you bring to an interview is still more precise than showing up empty-handed. Each iteration with a real person gives real signal.

## T8: Counter — "AI research is inaccurate."
Yes. But when each iteration costs hours instead of months, you can afford to be wrong more often. 10 fast iterations with live market > 1 long perfect iteration.

## T9: Personal proof — the BOOST intensive.
Built it exactly this way: research in 1.5-2 hours, MVP in a couple hours, Stripe integration in 3 hours. Selling the next day. First cohort sold out in 24 hours. Second cohort too. The developer didn't even know it was happening.
