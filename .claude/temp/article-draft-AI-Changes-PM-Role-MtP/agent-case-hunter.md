## THESIS A1: Business Judgment as the Key Differentiator

*When AI makes execution cheap, judgment = main differentiator*

### Case 1.1: Ravi Mehta -- "From Craft to Judgment" (Reforge)

**Who:** Ravi Mehta, ex-CPO at Tinder, former PM at Facebook and TripAdvisor; co-creator of Reforge AI Strategy Program
**What happened:** Mehta articulated the fundamental shift: historically, creating something required taste (knowing what to make) and craft (skill to make it). As AI democratizes craft, anyone can produce something polished. What's now scarce is knowing what to create and why it matters. He built the Reforge AI Strategy program around this thesis, calling it "the most important cohort yet" (Spring 2025). His framework: AI doesn't eliminate PM -- it elevates what has always differentiated exceptional PMs from average ones. Strategic vision, judgment, taste, and leadership become dramatically more valuable as traditional PM workflows get automated.
**Numbers:** Reforge program described as critical response to "the most intense environment in the history of tech"
**Why it matters:** Provides the conceptual framework for A1 -- judgment > craft in the AI era
**Source:** https://www.atlassian.com/blog/artificial-intelligence/shift-from-craft-to-judgement-ai / https://www.reforge.com/blog/ai-impact-product-management
**Year:** 2025

### Case 1.2: Marty Cagan -- AI Exposes "PM Theater" (SVPG)

**Who:** Marty Cagan, founder of SVPG, author of Inspired/Empowered/Transformed
**What happened:** In February 2026, Cagan published a landmark article on Product Coaching and AI representing "a significant change to what SVPG has been advocating for the past two decades." His key insight: if your CEO and engineers decide your role is simply "product management theater" (writing PRDs, maintaining roadmaps, running ceremonies), your job IS in jeopardy. Ironically, AI exposes this theater rather than highlighting potential contribution. The PM who moves tasks in a tracker will be replaced. The PM who owns outcomes will become MORE essential. SVPG recalibrated: AI forces PM-s to become outcome-leaders, not artifact-producers.
**Numbers:** 20+ years of SVPG methodology recalibrated around AI
**Why it matters:** The most authoritative voice in product management confirms: judgment and outcome-ownership are the only defensible PM skills
**Source:** https://www.svpg.com/product-coaching-and-ai/
**Year:** 2026

### Case 1.3: General Assembly PM Survey -- 98% Use AI, <50% Feel Prepared

**Who:** 117 product managers surveyed across US, UK, Canada, Singapore (companies with 100+ employees)
**What happened:** General Assembly ran a survey (Oct 2-13, 2025) revealing a paradox: 98% of PMs use AI at work, averaging 11 AI tool interactions per day (top 10% use it 25 times daily). 78% already use AI agents. Yet only 39% say they've received comprehensive, job-specific AI training. Less than half feel prepared to use AI effectively. Two-thirds (66%) admit using unapproved "shadow AI" tools. This illustrates A1: PMs adopted AI for execution, but lack the judgment framework to use it strategically.
**Numbers:** 98% AI adoption, 11x/day usage, 78% use AI agents, only 39% trained, 66% shadow AI
**Why it matters:** Proves the gap between AI adoption (execution) and AI mastery (judgment) -- exactly the thesis of A1
**Source:** https://generalassemb.ly/blog/ai-and-product-management-survey/
**Year:** 2025

## THESIS A2: PMs Become Full-Stack Builders

*Vibe coding, "Mexican standoff" of roles, T-shaped to E/F-shaped*

### Case 2.1: LinkedIn Kills APM Program, Creates "Full-Stack Builder" (Tomer Cohen)

**Who:** Tomer Cohen, Chief Product Officer at LinkedIn (14 years, left Jan 2026)
**What happened:** LinkedIn scrapped its traditional Associate Product Manager program and replaced it with an Associate Product Builder program that teaches coding, design, and PM skills together. LinkedIn introduced a formal "Full Stack Builder" title and career ladder, enabling anyone from any function to take products from idea to launch. Cohen reorganized teams into small "pods" of cross-trained builders, replacing large function-split groups. His stated goal: builders with "vision, empathy, communication, creativity, and judgment -- especially the ability to make high-quality decisions in complex, ambiguous situations" while automating "everything else." This appeared on Lenny's Podcast (Dec 2025).

## THESIS A3: PM as Hypothesis Pipeline Operator

*Prompt sets instead of PRDs, conveyor of hypotheses, small teams*

### Case 3.1: Aparna Chennapragada -- "Prompt Sets Are the New PRDs" (Microsoft)

**Who:** Aparna Chennapragada, Chief Product Officer of Experiences & Devices at Microsoft
**What happened:** Chennapragada introduced the concept "Prompt Sets Are the New PRDs" -- at Microsoft, when teams move forward with a new project or feature, they must provide an interactive prototype AND a collection of prompts, not just traditional documentation. Prompts function as "part specification, part training data" for agent behavior. She insists: "if you're building new projects, new features -- of course come with prototypes and prompt sets." Her broader thesis: "NLX (Natural Language Experience) is the new UX." Microsoft is building planned agents (Researcher, Analyst, Project Manager) within M365 Copilot based on this approach.
**Numbers:** Microsoft's entire M365 Copilot product development shifted to prompt-set specifications
**Why it matters:** The CPO of the world's most valuable company formalizing prompt sets as the new spec format -- directly validates A3
**Source:** https://aparnacd.substack.com/p/prompt-sets-are-the-new-prds / https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/microsoft-cpo-on-ai
**Year:** 2025

## THESIS A4: Full Cycle to Production -- 12-24 Months, Critical Chain Bottleneck

*Prototype is easy, production is hard; verification gap*

### Case 4.1: Veracode Report -- 45% of AI-Generated Code Has Security Vulnerabilities

**Who:** Veracode (AppSec leader), analyzing 80 coding tasks across 100+ LLMs
**What happened:** Veracode's 2025 GenAI Code Security Report revealed that 45% of AI-generated code samples failed security tests, introducing OWASP Top 10 vulnerabilities. Critical finding: while models improved at writing functionally correct code, security performance remained FLAT regardless of model size or training sophistication. Java had a 72% security failure rate; Python, C#, JavaScript ranged 38-45%. Cross-Site Scripting defenses failed in 86% of relevant samples. This means the critical chain from prototype to production remains firmly in place -- AI makes building fast but doesn't remove the security/quality bottleneck.
**Numbers:** 45% security failure rate, 72% Java failure, 86% XSS defense failure, 100+ LLMs tested, 80 coding tasks
**Why it matters:** Hard evidence that the critical chain (prototype -> production) is NOT shortened by AI; verification is the new bottleneck
**Source:** https://www.veracode.com/resources/analyst-reports/2025-genai-code-security-report/ / https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250730694951/en/
**Year:** 2025

---

### Case 4.2: Sonar "Verification Gap" -- 96% Don't Trust, Only 48% Verify

**Who:** Sonar (code quality platform), surveying developers
**What happened:** Sonar's research uncovered the critical "Verification Gap": 96% of developers do NOT fully trust that AI-generated code is functionally correct, yet only 48% always check their AI-assisted code before committing. AWS CTO Werner Vogels termed this "verification debt." Additional data: code duplication increased 48%, refactoring activity dropped 60%. Forrester predicts 50%+ of tech decision-makers face moderate-to-severe technical debt by 2025, rising to 75% by 2026.
**Numbers:** 96% don't trust AI code, 48% verify, 48% duplication increase, 60% refactoring drop
**Why it matters:** The production bottleneck is real and growing -- speed of generation >> speed of verification. This IS the critical chain.
**Source:** https://www.sonarsource.com/company/press-releases/sonar-data-reveals-critical-verification-gap-in-ai-coding/ / https://www.sonarsource.com/blog/how-ai-is-redefining-technical-debt/
**Year:** 2025

### Case 4.3: Klarna -- AI Triumph Turns to "Quality Crisis" and Human Rehiring

**Who:** Klarna (global payments), CEO Sebastian Siemiatkowski
**What happened:** In February 2024, Klarna's AI assistant managed 2.3 million customer conversations in its first month -- two-thirds of all customer service. Equivalent to 700 full-time agents. Resolution time dropped from 11 to 2 minutes. 25% drop in repeat inquiries. Projected $40M profit improvement. Cost: $2-3M to deploy. BUT by mid-2025, the story reversed: Siemiatkowski admitted "cost was a predominant evaluation factor" resulting in "lower quality." Klarna began rehiring human agents. The AI assistant dropped to ~1.3M conversations/month. This is the best cautionary tale for A4: fast AI deployment without production-quality judgment leads to regression.
**Numbers:** 2.3M conversations/month (peak), 700 FTE equivalent, 11 -> 2 min resolution, $40M projected improvement, then quality regression and rehiring
**Why it matters:** Perfect A4 illustration -- prototype-level success (AI handles 2/3 of volume) followed by production-level failure (quality drops, humans rehired)
**Source:** https://www.klarna.com/international/press/klarna-ai-assistant-handles-two-thirds-of-customer-service-chats-in-its-first-month/ / https://www.customerexperiencedive.com/news/klarna-reinvests-human-talent-customer-service-AI-chatbot/747586/
**Year:** 2024-2025
