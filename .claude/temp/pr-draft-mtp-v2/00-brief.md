## Core idea
AI is transforming the PM role from feature coordinator to full-stack builder with business judgment. Speed without judgment is the feature mill on steroids. The PM market is splitting K-shaped.

## Theses (from Ivan's current draft v2 — PRESERVE THIS LOGIC)

### Thesis 1: The builder era is here
Full-stack builder PM is real. Boris Cherny (Claude Code) — 100% AI code since Nov 2025, +200% productivity. YC W25: 25% startups with 95% AI code. Andreessen: "Mexican standoff" PM/eng/design. LinkedIn: "Associate Product Builder". Reframe: full-stack builder ≠ PM who learned to code. It's PM who tests dozens of hypotheses across segments, value props, activation, business models — and maintains production quality.

### Thesis 2: Speed without judgment is the feature mill on steroids
When AI crushes execution cost → bottleneck moves to business judgment. Diagnosing where in causal chain growth sits: market segment → JTBD → strategy → value prop → unit economics → channels. Cagan: AI makes "PM theater" replaceable. Mehta: shift from craft to judgment. Without judgment, vibe coding = feature mill on steroids. 90% of resources go to features that never find market. AI makes that 10x faster.

### Thesis 3: From PRDs to hypothesis pipelines
AI collapses cost of building → test 10-100x more hypotheses per sprint. Not launching products — buying knowledge. PM = operator of hypothesis factory. Boris Cherny: "Claude is starting to come up with ideas." Shreyas Doshi: 3-4 person startups = impact of 30-50 teams. Delegation follows risk: AI agents = less competent employees, hand them low-risk tasks.

### Thesis 4: The full cycle isn't here yet
Prototype in afternoon ≠ ship to production in afternoon. Critical chain breaks at security, DevOps, compliance. Each component multiplies probability of failure. Full cycle 12-24 months away. Kill criteria, reversibility, circuit breakers.

### Thesis 5: The split is already happening
K-shaped PM market. WEF: 94% leaders face skill shortages in AI-ready roles, 92% report 20% workforce overcapacity. Builder PMs pulled forward, traditional PMs pushed back. Habit deeply embedded, fear of coding real. People overestimate readiness to change by 3x. Innovator's dilemma in real time. Not "adapt or die" but "build the environment that lets people adapt." Bifurcation = architecture, not talent.

### Close: Where to start
1. Build one prototype this week (to learn, not ship)
2. Find growth points in business model
3. Find risks in backlog: "If this assumption is wrong, what do we lose?"
4. Talk to one customer this week
