## Section Brief: Section 1 — "The One Decision That Matters" (~300 words)

**Thesis:** 1 — Product strategy = choosing customer jobs. Wrong choice = #1 failure cause.

### SECTION SKELETON (thesis body):
Everything in a product — value, features, landing pages, acquisition, retention — flows from knowing your customers' jobs. A rigid causal chain exists: Market → Segments → Jobs → Value → Communication → Conversion → Profit. An error at any earlier step breaks everything downstream. The vast majority of startups choose small, difficult, less profitable segments. Three catastrophic errors: (1) Product for non-existent jobs of non-existent people. (2) Working on unprofitable segments — a larger one may exist nearby. (3) Spreading resources — features for everyone = value for no one. CB Insights (431 startups): 43% of failures = poor product-market fit.

### MUST-INCLUDE ELEMENTS:
**DATA:** CB Insights 43% of 431 startups fail from poor PMF. Median last round to shutdown: 22 months.
**QUOTE:** Bob Moesta: "People don't buy products. They hire them to do a job." — [URL](https://saasclub.io/podcast/jobs-to-be-done-bob-moesta-423/)
**CONCEPT:** Causal chain Market → Segments → Jobs → Value → Communication → Conversion → Profit. Three catastrophic segmentation errors.

### CASE:
**Ivan's case:** ArtDent (dentistry) — 22 interviews, found highest-margin segment "fix all teeth in one day," revenue +37%. Source: `06-cases.md`
**External:** CB Insights data (431 startups, 43%). [URL](https://www.cbinsights.com/research/report/startup-failure-reasons-top/)

### TRANSITION to Section 2:

## Section Brief: Section 2 — "The Wrong Job Trap" (~250 words)

**Thesis:** 2 — Companies compete for wrong segment/jobs → opportunity cost.

### SECTION SKELETON (thesis body):
Companies choose among customers who have many jobs, and the product competes for a small number of them — often not the most optimal. A larger, more solvent segment with higher-frequency jobs may exist nearby — but the founder doesn't know about it. Trying to serve two conflicting jobs simultaneously ("bipolar product problem") tears the product apart: InVideo spent 3 years serving beginners and experts → after JTBD-driven rejection of experts, growth from $0 to $25M in 6 months. Every time a founder builds a feature instead of seeing the full job graph, they incur opportunity cost.

### MUST-INCLUDE ELEMENTS:
**DATA:** InVideo $0→$25M in 6 months.
**QUOTE:** Bob Moesta: "You end up building a product that tries to do everything for everybody, and it ends up doing nothing for nobody." — [URL](https://saasclub.io/podcast/jobs-to-be-done-bob-moesta-423/)
**CONCEPT:** Bipolar product problem. Opportunity cost thinking.

### CASE:
**External (primary):** InVideo — 3 years stuck, fired expert segment, $0→$25M in 6 months. [URL](https://saasclub.io/podcast/jobs-to-be-done-bob-moesta-423/)
**Ivan's case (supporting):** LiFT — doing "everything for everyone" at 150K/month, shifted to C-level executives → check x3, revenue +65%. Source: `06-cases.md`
