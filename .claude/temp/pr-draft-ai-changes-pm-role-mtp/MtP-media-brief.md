# Media brief: Mind the Product

**Outlet:** Mind the Product — https://www.mindtheproduct.com  
**Word count:** 1000–1800 (target ~1300)  
**Language:** English only  
**Format:** Practical, actionable — what PMs can do Monday morning. Opinion + evidence.

## Tone
- Direct, experienced, first-person where it fits. Like Ivan in person.
- Smart but accessible. Not academic. Not corporate. Not AI-sounding.
- Mind the Product audience: PMs, product leads, founders. They want concrete takeaways.

## Structure
- Strong hook (personal or surprising stat).
- Clear sections with subheads.
- End with actionable list (e.g. 3–5 things to do this week).
- No generic “in conclusion” — land the point.

## Banned / avoid
- “In today's rapidly evolving landscape”
- “It's worth noting that” / “It is important to note”
- “Delve into”, “Leverage” (metaphorical), “Game-changer”, “Paradigm shift”, “Groundbreaking”
- “Navigating the complexities of”, “In the realm of”
- “Importantly,” / “Notably,” / “Crucially,” as sentence starters
- Three subordinate clauses in a row; passive where active is better
- Hedging stacks: “might potentially”, “could possibly”, “may perhaps”
- “It is essential to understand that”, “This article will explore”

## Suggested case phrases (use naturally)
- “In my experience across multiple product teams…”
- “We tried this. It didn’t work. Here’s why.”
- “Most product managers get this backwards.”

## Special requirements
- All external claims need real URLs.
- Ivan’s own cases: cite repository or “in my practice” where relevant.
